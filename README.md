# Sales Performance Analytics & Strategic Reporting

# Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of multi-regional sales data to identify growth drivers, seasonal trends, and product performance. By leveraging Python for data preprocessing and Power BI for visualization, the project transforms raw transactional records into actionable business intelligence.

 # Key Objectives
Data Cleaning: Processing raw Excel datasets to ensure consistency across regions and categories.
Trend Analysis: Identifying high-velocity sales periods and seasonal peaks.
Regional Benchmarking: Evaluating performance across West, East, Central, and South zones.
Product Strategy: Categorizing products by revenue impact to support inventory and marketing decisions.

# Tech Stack
Data Processing: Microsoft Excel (CSV)
Business Intelligence: Microsoft Power BI
Documentation: Markdown

# Repository Structure
Final dataset.xlsx - CleanData.csv: The refined dataset used for the final analysis.
task 1 (BI).pbix: The interactive Power BI dashboard containing regional and product-wise visualizations.
Data Sheets/: Supplemental CSV files containing pivot summaries for Sales by Product, Region, and Monthly Trends.

# Executive Insights
Based on the analysis of over $2.29M in total sales:
Top Regions: The West ($725k) and East ($678k) regions lead in market penetration.
Core Categories: Phones and Chairs are the primary revenue anchors, totaling over $650k combined.
Seasonality: A significant sales surge is observed in Q4, with peaks in September, November, and December.

# Business Recommendations
Inventory Optimization: Increase stock levels for high-demand items (Phones/Chairs) ahead of the September surge.
Market Expansion: Implement targeted promotional strategies in the South region to bridge the revenue gap.
Seasonal Marketing: Align advertising spend with the identified year-end peak months to maximize ROI.
Portfolio Management: Review low-velocity categories (Fasteners/Labels) for potential bundling strategies.

# How to Use
View Data: Open the CleanData.csv to view the processed transactional records.
Interactive Dashboard: Download the task 1 (BI).pbix file and open it in Power BI Desktop to explore the interactive visualizations and filters.
